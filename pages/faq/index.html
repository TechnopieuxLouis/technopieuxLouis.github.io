FAQ's

1. What is a helical pile? The Techno Metal Post is a metal pile used as a foundation and works like a screw. It consists of a tube, a helical shaft at the bottom, and a plate at the top. The helix serves two purposes: it allows the pile to be screwed into the ground and provides load-bearing support. A helical pile is a steel foundation installed into the ground by screwing it in using specialised equipment. The top plate, which comes in several standard sizes, is precisely positioned at the required height and supports the load-bearing parts of the structure. The Techno Metal Post is available in various tube sizes and helix diameters. 2. Do I need a soil test? In general, most residential or light commercial projects do not require a soil test. Techno Metal Post doesn’t provide soil testing, but can perform a Pile Test (torque reading test) to determine the necessary depth to reach proper bearing capacity and the size of the helix required for a specific project. In some places, regional policies dictate that a soil test must be done (so check with the authorities in your area). 3. Why use a helical pile foundation instead of concrete? Using helical piles will save you time and money, since they don’t require any excavation, concrete curing time or site repairs. Also, ensuring your project’s stability will prevent additional costs resulting from probable soil movement. 4. What types of projects can Techno Metal Posts be used for? Techno Metal Posts are suitable for a wide range of projects: decks, extensions, houses, commercial buildings,modulars, docks, fences, and more. They work well in all kinds of soil, even unstable ground. 5. Can I install Techno Metal Posts myself? Our helical piles can only be installed by our certified installers, using our specially designed machines. 6. How long does it take to install? Installation time depends on the project type. It mainly varies based on the number of piles, soil conditions, and the site access situation. 7. Is it possible to install helical piles in rocky soil conditions? Our installation equipment can screw piles through soil containing small to moderate sized rocks. Our machines generate the appropriate torque for the pile to move these rocks. If necessary, the functions of the telescopic and mobile mast will be used to "steer" the pile around the rock during installation. In the case of shallow bedrock or larger rocks, a specialized anchoring system can be used. 8. What is the minimum access width you need for the installation equipment? Our smallest machine, the R2D, can easily maneuver through a standard door frame (29 inches / 75 cm). 9. Do you have any certifications? Yes. Our products meet current international standards and hold BBA, CE, ISO 9001, and UKCA certifications. This ensures safety, quality, and regulatory compliance for our clients. 10. Is it possible to install piles in winter when the ground is frozen? We can install piles year-round, including winter.Thanks to our specialised equipment, we can install the piles even in winter conditions or on frozen ground, making this method especially effective all year round. 11. What is the maximum load that a pile can support? The load-bearing capacity of a Techno Metal Post cannot simply be guessed - it must be calculated, planned for and adapted to each project. There are two key elements involved: the model of the pile and the installation depth. 12. The diameter and strength of the pile Our engineers have created a pile selection table to help you choose the appropriate pile model, based on the loads being supported. This process ensures that each pile is properly proportioned to the project's specific requirements. 13. Installation depth and load-bearing capacity of the soil Each pile is installed by one of our certified professionals, in accordance with the soil’s properties. Our installers follow a correlation chart to confirm that the pile's load-bearing capacity is appropriate for the type of soil encountered. 14.
